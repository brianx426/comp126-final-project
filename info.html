General Information

Geography and Demographics

Kagawa Prefecture (香川県) is located on the northeast portion on the island of Shikoku. Kagawa Prefecture has a population of 949,358 people. It is the smallest prefecture by geographic area at 1,877 square kilometers. Kagawa Prefecture's neighbors are Ehime Prefecture (愛媛県) to the southwest and Tokushima Prefecture (徳島県) to the south. To the north lies the Seto Inland Sea. Beyond that lies Okayama Prefecture (岡山県). Okayama Prefecture and Kagawa Prefecture are connected by the Great Seto Bridge. Kagawa Prefecture's capital is Takamatsu, which also happens to be the largest city of the prefecture.

History

Before the modern prefectures was established, Kagawa Prefecture was known as Sanuki Province. During the Meiji Era, specifically in August of 1876, Kagawa was made a part of Ehime Prefecture. In December of 1888, Kagawa regained its independent prefecture status.

Food

Kagawa is famous for many foods! The most famous of them all is the Sanuki Udon, a style of thick wheat noodles that became a cultural identity for the area. Kagawa is also famous for "hone-tsuki-dori", seasoned chicken thighs cooked on the bone. The dish originated in Marugame City, here in Kagawa! Now it has become a popular dish across izakayas in Japan! Other staples include olive products, such as olive beef and olive yellowtail, and delectable sweets made from locally produced wasanbon sugar.

Transportation

Kagawa Prefecture is well-connected by both land and sea. One of the most impressive landmarks in Kagawa, the Great Seto Bridge, links Kagawa to Okayama Prefecture on Honshu. The bridge serves both cars and trains, making it an essential route for travel and commerce. Within the prefecture, the JR Shikoku rail lines and various local trains provide easy access to cities and towns. Ferries connect Takamatsu and other coastal cities to nearby islands like Naoshima and Shōdoshima, making it easy to explore the Seto Inland Sea. Takamatsu Airport offers domestic flights to major cities like Tokyo and Okinawa, and also a few international flights to destinations such as Seoul and Shanghai.

Weather and Climate

The climate in Kagawa is mild and relatively dry. Kagawa receives some of the lowest annual rainfall in Japan. Summers here are warm but not excessively humid. Winters are cool with very little snowfall. Quite pleasant! The mild climate and pleasant weather make Kagawa a great place for outdoor activities all year-round. This is perfect since Kagawa possesses awesome outdoor attractions!

Fun Facts

  • Smallest Prefecture in Japan
  • Nicknamed the "Udon Kingdom" for the presence of over 600 udon shops in Kagawa
  • First place in Japan to cultivate olives
  • Home to Takamatsu Castle, one of only three Japanese castles built with a moat
  • The famous Kotohira-gu (Shinto Shrine) has 785 stone steps to the main shrine and 1,368 steps to the innner shrine
  • 90% of all uchiwa fans (traditional hand fans) in Japan are made in Marugame
  • Scenic spots throughout Kagawa have made appearances in many anime and live-action films
  • There are "Udon Taxis" that will take you to the best udon shops around
