Setonaikai National Park (瀬戸内海国立公園)

Quick Facts

Setonaikai National Park, established in 1934, is Japan’s first national park and one of the country’s most scenic natural treasures. Spanning across multiple prefectures and over 3,000 islands in the Seto Inland Sea, this vast park offers breathtaking coastal views, rich biodiversity, and a harmonious blend of land and sea. Its mild climate, calm waters, and unique island landscapes have inspired poets, artists, and travelers for centuries.

Activities

Setonaikai National Park offers endless opportunities to explore nature, culture, and adventure. Whether by land or sea, there’s something for every traveler to enjoy!


  1. Hike or cycle along the Shimanami Kaido, a scenic 60-kilometer route connecting Honshu and Shikoku across a chain of islands. Enjoy panoramic sea views and charming island towns along the way!
  2. Explore the many beaches and viewpoints on islands like Miyajima, Naoshima, and Shodoshima, each with its own character and cultural sites.
  3. Take a ferry ride through the tranquil waters of the Seto Inland Sea and admire the countless small islands dotting the landscape.
  4. Visit historic temples, shrines, and fishing villages tucked into the hills and coastlines, offering a glimpse into traditional life along the sea.
