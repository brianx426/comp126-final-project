Naoshima (直島)

Quick Facts

Naoshima is a small island located in the Seto Inland Sea, renowned for its unique fusion of contemporary art, architecture, and tranquil natural scenery. Often called “Art Island,” Naoshima is home to world-famous museums, outdoor installations, and architectural masterpieces by Tadao Ando. The island’s peaceful atmosphere and artistic ambiance make it a one-of-a-kind destination in Japan.

Activities

Naoshima offers a variety of art-centered experiences and scenic relaxation. Explore modern creativity surrounded by stunning coastal beauty!


  1. Visit the Chichu Art Museum, an underground museum designed by Tadao Ando. It houses works by Claude Monet, James Turrell, and Walter De Maria—all beautifully integrated with natural light.
  2. Stroll through the Benesse House Museum and its surrounding art installations scattered across the island, where art and nature seamlessly blend together.
  3. Discover the Art House Project in Honmura, where traditional Japanese houses have been transformed into fascinating contemporary art spaces.
  4. Take photos with Yayoi Kusama’s iconic pumpkin sculptures, located along the coastline—perfect for a memorable seaside experience.
